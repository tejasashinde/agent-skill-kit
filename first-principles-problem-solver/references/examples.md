# First Principles Real World Examples

## Example 1: SpaceX Rocket
When Elon Musk wanted to go to Mars, he found that buying a finished rocket cost $65 million. Instead of accepting that price, he broke the rocket down into its "first principles"—the raw materials like aerospace-grade aluminum, titanium, and carbon fiber. He realized the cost of those materials was only about 2% of the total rocket price. By building the technology from scratch instead of buying it, SpaceX was able to cut the cost of a launch by nearly 10x.

## Example 2: Apple iPod
Before the iPod, "reasoning by analogy" meant making CD players smaller or more skip-resistant. Apple used first principles to ask: "What is the fundamental goal?" The answer was "carrying a music library everywhere." Instead of tweaking the CD player, they looked at the core components—miniature hard drives and battery technology—to build a device that held 1,000 songs in your pocket, completely ignoring the "best practices" of the disc-based industry.

## Principle Applied
- Always break things down to raw components
- Always ignore existing industry standard approaches
- Always calculate what should be physically possible
- Never accept price or limitations just because everyone else does
